Your First Audio Recording

In this tutorial, you'll learn how to record audio for the first time. We will guide you through adding an audio track, configuring your audio input, setting up monitoring, using the metronome, and understanding the pre-click function.

Learning Goals:

  • Learn how to add an audio track.
  • Learn how to configure your audio input.
  • Learn how to monitor your audio.
  • Learn about the metronome.
  • Learn about the count-in.

Part 1: Adding an Audio Track

To start recording audio, you first need to add an audio track to your project.

Steps to Add an Audio Track:

Create a New Project:

  • Launch Cubase and create a new project by choosing an empty project or a suitable template

Add an Audio Track:

  • In the Project window, click on the + button on top of the track list or press T.
  • Select the Audio icon in the top left corner.

In the Add Track dialog:

  • To ensure your audio is recorded correctly, you need to configure the audio input for your track.
  • Click on the dropdown menu and select the Audio Input that corresponds to your audio interface (e.g., Mono In 1 or Stereo In 1).
  • Choose a Mono or Stereo configuration based on your recording source. Single vocals are usually recorded with one microphone in Mono while drums or any kind of ensemble is usually recorded in stereo. If you are recording your guitar by plugging it directly in your audio interface, it will be recorded in mono.
  • Name your track (e.g., "Vocal" or "Guitar").
  • Click Add Track.

Check Input Levels:

  • Ensure your microphone or instrument is connected to the selected input on your audio interface.
  • Play or speak into your microphone to check the input levels in the MixConsole (press F3 to open the MixConsole). You will see the input on the left side of the MixConsole. You won't hear the input signal through Cubase at this point, but you can adjust the gain on your audio interface to avoid that your signal is too loud or too low by checking the Meter.

Part 2: Monitoring Your Audio

Monitoring allows you to hear what you are recording in real-time.

Steps to Set Up Monitoring:

Enable Monitoring:

  • Click the Monitor button on the audio track. It looks like a small speaker icon and will turn orange when activated.
  • You should now hear the input signal through your headphones or speakers.

Adjust Monitoring Settings:

  • Go to Studio > Studio Setup and select your audio interface.
  • Ensure the Direct Monitoring option is enabled if your audio interface supports it. This reduces latency during recording.

Part 3: Using the Metronome

The metronome provides a click track to help you stay in time during recording. The speed and rhythm of the metronome are determined by the tempo and time signature of your project.

To enable the Metronome click the Metronome button in the Transport Bar. It will light up when enabled. Alternatively, press Con your keyboard to toggle the metronome on and off.

Understand the Metronome Timing:

Understand the Metronome Timing:

  • The tempo (BPM) controls how fast the metronome clicks.
  • The time signature determines the rhythmic pattern of the clicks. For example: In 4/4 time, the metronome will click four times per measure (one for each beat). In 3/4 time, it will click three times per measure (like a waltz).

Part 4: Understanding the Count-In

The count-in provides a timing-reference before the recording starts, allowing you to prepare. It plays a set number of clicks before the actual recording begins.

Steps to Use the Count-In:

Enable the Count-In:

  • Go to Transport > Metronome Setup.
  • In the Count-In section, check the Click During Count-In box.
  • Set the Count-In Bars to the number of bars you need before recording starts (e.g., 1 or 2 bars).

Activate Count-In in Transport Bar:

  • In the Transport Bar, click on the three dots (...) to expand additional options.
  • Click the Count-In button (a metronome with a "/" icon). It will light up when activated.
  • Click the When you press Record, you’ll hear the pre-click count-in before the actual recording starts. (a metronome with a "/" icon). It will light up when activated.

Part 5: Recording Your First Audio

Now that everything is set up, you're ready to record.

Steps to Record Audio:

Prepare for Recording:

  • Ensure the track is selected and the Record Enable button (red circle) is activated on the track.
  • Position the playhead where you want to start recording.

Start Recording:

  • Press Record in the Transport Bar or press *** (asterisk)** on the numeric keypad.
  • Play or sing your part.

Stop Recording:

  • Press Stop in the Transport Bar or press Spacebar when you’re finished.

Review Your Recording:

  • The recorded audio will appear as an event in the track.
  • Make sure that the Monitor button on the track is deactivated. It shouldn't be orange any more.
  • Play it back to review by positioning the playhead at the start of the recording and pressing Play.

Example: Let's Record a Vocal Track

Let’s record a vocal track step by step.

Set Up the Project:

  • Create a new project, set the tempo to 120 BPM, and add a mono audio track named "Vocal".

Configure the Input:

  • When adding the track, set the input routing to Mono In 1 in the Add Track dialogue (assuming your microphone is connected to input 1 on your audio interface).

Use Headphones to Avoid Feedback:

  • Before recording, put on headphones to prevent the microphone from picking up sound from your speakers. This avoids feedback and ensures a clean vocal recording.

Enable Monitoring:

  • Click the Monitor button on the vocal track to hear your microphone.

Check input level:

  • Make sure that the signal is not too loud or too low by checking the meter on the input bus in the MixConsole. Adjust the gain on your audio interface if needed.

Use the Metronome:

  • Enable the metronome by clicking the metronome icon in the Transport Bar.

Set Up Count-In:

  • Enable Count-In and set it to 1 bar in the Metronome Setup.

Start Recording:

  • Arm the vocal track by clicking the Record Enable button.
  • Position the playhead at the beginning and press Record.
  • Sing your part, then press Stop when done.

Review the Recording:

  • Play back the recording to ensure it was captured correctly.

Tips for Successful Audio Recording

Check Levels: Always check input levels before recording to avoid clipping.

Minimize Noise: Record in a quiet environment and use pop filters for vocals to minimize unwanted noise.

Practice with Pre-Click: Use the pre-click to get comfortable with the tempo before the actual recording starts.

Monitor Comfortably: Ensure your monitoring levels are comfortable and not too loud to avoid ear fatigue.

By following these steps, you'll be able to record your first audio track in Cubase with confidence. These fundamental skills will help you capture high-quality recordings for all your projects. Happy recording!
