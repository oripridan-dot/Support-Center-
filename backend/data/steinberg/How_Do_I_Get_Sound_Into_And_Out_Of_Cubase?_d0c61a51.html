How Do I Get Sound Into And Out Of Cubase?

If your sound isn’t flowing into or out of Cubase as you want, this guide will help you to fix it.

Quick Tips

Here’s a quick grab bag of things to check if you aren’t getting sound into or out of Cubase.

  • First, make sure that the correct input and output device is selected in the Audio Connections and Studio Setup windows.
  • Next, in the Device Port column, click to make sure your main outputs are being sent to outputs 1 and 2 on the device, where your speakers should be connected.
  • For troubleshooting audio input, check that inputs 1 and 2 are activated in the Input column.
  • Make sure that the tracks you are listening to are not muted.
  • Then, check to see if your master fader set is too low.
  • Ensure that your headphone or speaker levels are not set too low on the hardware.
  • Finally, ensure that your audio interface’s software control panel does not have its level set too low.

Cubase is the perfect way to capture your musical performances and ideas. It takes sound from microphones and instruments and converts it to digital audio which is recorded to your computer’s hard drive. The sounds you record need to be sent through an audio interface which acts as a converter from analog to digital sound, and also lets you connect monitor speakers and headphones for listening back to your recordings.

It may not necessarily always be clear to those who are new to digital recording how this works. Where are you supposed to connect your guitar for example, and what happens if the sound doesn’t seem to be making it through into Cubase? If you can’t hear your track through your headphones, where can you look to fix the issue? How can you make sure your levels are correct? In this guide we’ll answer these questions and have you up and running with recording in Cubase in no time.

Setting Up Audio Connections

Begin by connecting your audio interface to your computer – it might be something like the Steinberg UR22C. Download and install the drivers to make it work if they are required, they probably will be if you are using a Windows computer but may not be needed if you are on a Mac.

Start up Cubase and create a new project. Then go to the Studio menu from the top menu bar on the screen and from there select Studio Setup. You might see “built-in audio” selected. Select the Audio System option then on the right, click on the ASIO Driver menu. From there, select the name of your audio interface. Cubase will switch to using the interface for audio in and out.

Returning to the Devices list on the left, click on the name of your interface. In the Ports section on the right, all ports should be active and shown with a tick but if not you can tick them to activate them.

Click OK to exit this menu and then go to the Studio menu at the top of the screen again and this time choose Audio Connections. In the Audio Connections window, go to the Outputs tab and under Audio Devices, make sure your interface is selected.

In the Device Port column, click to make sure your main outputs are being sent to outputs 1 and 2 on the device, where your speakers should be connected. Then in the Inputs tab do the same and this time, make sure inputs 1 and 2 are selected. Close this window and now you’re ready to test the sound.

Select Project > Add Track > Audio from the menu at the top of the screen and choose a mono or stereo track.

Go to the new track and click its monitor button – this looks like a small speaker – and play your instrument or speak into the mic. You should see signal coming through on the level meters. If you don’t see it, go back and check the previous steps to see that the correct interface and inputs are selected, and check the gain level on the audio interface is up high enough.

Different Ways Of Listening

If you are recording an instrument that doesn’t need a microphone, say a directly connected electric guitar or a synth, you can listen on speakers. And if you are using VST instruments inside Cubase you can use speakers as well. Speakers should be connected to the main stereo outputs of your audio interface, and selected in the Audio Connections > Outputs tab as described above.

If you are recording using a microphone you will need to turn your speakers off so that the sound doesn’t feed back. Connect headphones to the headphone output on your audio interface and the output of Cubase will be sent to them. If you are using Cubase with your computer’s built-in soundcard, you can connect headphones directly to the computer’s headphone port.

What If Cubase Is Too Quiet Or Too Loud?

If you find your levels seem too quiet or too loud, try the following. For inputs, start with the gain control on your instrument, then check the gain control on the input channel of your interface – this also applies for microphones. They should be set somewhere from 75%-90% but ideally not at 100%. Then check the input fader on your audio track in Cubase, which should be set at or just under zero.

For outputs, again check the channel’s fader and also the Master Stereo Out fader which you can find in MixConsole my choosing Studio > MixConsole. This should also be set at or just below zero. If that still doesn’t help, check the headphone or output level controls on your audio interface, and finally the gain controls on your speakers, if they are powered speakers.

By checking these simple things you can make sure sound is flowing into and out of Cubase correctly, and get on with making great music!
