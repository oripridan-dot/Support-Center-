Let the Music Play

Download Cubase Welcome Tutorial Project

In this tutorial, we'll cover the basics of playing back your project. You'll learn how to start and stop playback, how to set locators to define specific sections, and how to use the cycle mode to loop a part of your project.

Learning Goals:

  • Learn how to playback your project.
  • Learn how to set locators and use cycle mode.

Part 1: Playing Back Your Project

Playing back your project is an essential part of the music production process. It allows you to hear your work in progress and make necessary adjustments.

Steps to Playback Your Project:

1. Open Your Project:

  • Launch Cubase and open the project you want to play back. If you don't have a project yet, you can load the "Cubase Welcome Tutorial".

2. Start Playback:

  • To start playback, press the Spacebar on your keyboard or click the Play button in the Transport Bar at the bottom of the screen.

3. Stop Playback:

  • To stop playback, press the Spacebar again or click the Stop button in the Transport Bar.

4. Move the Playhead:

  • Click anywhere in the ruler at the top of the Project window to move the playhead to a specific position.

5. Adjust the Playback Speed:

  • The default tempo in Cubase is 120bpm (beats per minute).

Part 2: Setting Locators

Locators define specific sections of your project, making it easier to navigate and edit specific parts.

Steps to Set Locators:

1. Locate the Left and Right Locators:

  • The left and right locators are displayed in the ruler at the top of the Project window. They appear as triangle markers.

2. Set the Locators:

  • Move the cursor on the top half of the ruler. The cursor will change to a pen icon. Now click and drag a new section on the ruler.
  • A even faster way is to make a selection in the project and just press P. The locators are now set to the start and end of your selection.

3. Moving the Locators:

  • You can simply move the Locators by dragging them on ruler bar. If you want to move both you can drag the area between the locators.

4. Using the Locators:

  • The area between the left and right locators is highlighted, indicating the selected range.
  • You can use this range for various functions, such as exporting or looping a specific section of your project.

Part 3: Loop with Cycle Mode

Cycle mode allows you to loop a specific section of your project, which is useful for focusing on and refining parts of your composition.

Steps to Use Cycle Mode:

1. Enable Cycle Mode:

  • Click the Cycle button in the Transport Bar. It's the button left of the Stop button.
  • You can also click in the area between the locators to activate the Cycle Mode.
  • When the Cycle Mode is active the area between in locators turns purple.

2. Set the Locators:

  • Ensure the left and right locators are set to define the section you want to loop.

3. Start Playback in Cycle Mode:

  • Press the Spacebar or click the Play button to start playback. Cubase will loop the section between the locators continuously.

4. Exit Cycle Mode:

  • Click the Cycle button again to disable cycle mode and stop the looping.

Tips for Effective Playback and Editing

Use Markers: Set markers in your project to quickly jump to different sections. Go to Project > Add Marker or press Insert on your keyboard.

Keyboard Shortcuts: Familiarize yourself with playback-related keyboard shortcuts (e.g., Spacebar for play/stop, P for setting the locators to the selection.)

Customize Transport Bar: Right-click the Transport Bar to customize its layout and show or hide specific controls to fit your workflow.

By mastering playback, setting locators, and using cycle mode in Cubase, you'll enhance your ability to navigate and refine your projects efficiently. These foundational skills are crucial for effective music production, allowing you to focus on the creative aspects of your work. Happy playing!
