Q&A with Robin Lobel

Hi Robin! Many congratulations on the new release of SpectraLayers 12. You are the creator of SpectraLayers and have been developing it for over 15 years now. What would you say is the main focus of the updates in this new version?

Thank you. The main focus was on improving and extending the capabilities of SpectraLayers both in the music and post-production domains, thanks largely to AI, as well as simplifying the workflow based on users’ suggestions.

Can you tell us about some of the most-requested new features and changes you have brought to SpectraLayers 12? What kinds of things had users had been asking for the most?

Since the releases of SpectraLayers 10 and 11, users are always looking for improvements with the Unmix Song module, so we have raised the quality once again! In addition to that, the Unmix Drums module now goes up to 6 stems, with better separation. On the post-production side, the quality of the Voice DeNoise, Unmix Noisy Speech and Unmix Transcription tools have been significantly increased, and users have been requesting the addition of modules like DePlosive, which is now part of SpectraLayers 12. There have also been requests for a more robust and easier to use Harmonics Selection tool and the ability to edit multiple clips at once (in particular in ARA mode), and I’m happy to say these have been implemented as well.

SpectraLayers is used at all levels of music and audio production and post-production. How does this new version help musicians and producers to take their creativity to the next level?

In addition to the expected and requested improvements, SpectraLayers 12 also introduces four new modules that will greatly help both user groups; Unmix Soundtrack can unmix audio from shows or podcasts into dialogue, effects and musics layers, while Unmix Instrument can take a couple of seconds from one instrument and attempt to extract it from the rest of the mix. The new Voice Enhance module can also turn poorly recorded speech into a near studio-quality recording thanks to the use of generative AI.

Will existing users find the transition to version 12 is seamless?

Coming from version 10 or 11, transitioning to version 12 is indeed seamless. It keeps all the UI improvements introduced in 11, and brings additional UI improvements such as keyword searching in most panels, a more user-friendly volume envelope and new, targeted Cut/Copy operations that make the workflow even more efficient.

SpectraLayers has already incorporated AI-powered modules and gains upgrades in the new version. How do you see AI benefitting SpectraLayers users — and the pro audio world — going forward?

AI has been unlocking possibilities that couldn’t have been envisioned even six years ago. For SpectraLayers users, that means more and more possibilities to unmix sound in different ways, and better and better sound restoration and recovery capabilities. Speaking more generally, generative AI - which is both a powerful and controversial topic — is also impacting the pro audio world more and more.

Can you give us any hints about the future direction of SpectraLayers?

Each new version of SpectraLayers is usually a mix of requested or expected features and surprise features made possible by the constant evolution of technology. In the future, SpectraLayers will continue to explore AI capabilities in both expected and unexpected ways, as well as streamlining its workflow to make it more and more accessible — all while maintaining its powerful toolset and the philosophy that makes it unique, giving users an unmatched level of control over the sound.
