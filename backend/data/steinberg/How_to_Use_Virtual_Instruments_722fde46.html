How to Use Virtual Instruments

Download Cubase Welcome Tutorial Project

Welcome to Cubase! In this tutorial, we'll cover the basics of using virtual instruments. You'll learn about what virtual instruments are, how to load a VST instrument, and how to record a VST instrument.

Learning Goals:

  • Learn about virtual instruments.
  • Learn how to load a VST instrument.
  • Learn how to record a VST instrument.

Part 1: Understanding Virtual Instruments

Virtual instruments are software-based instruments that emulate real or synthesized sounds. They can be played using MIDI data and provide a wide range of sounds, from pianos and drums to synthesizers and orchestral instruments.

Common Types of Virtual Instruments:

  • Synthesizers: Generate a variety of synthetic sounds.
  • Samplers: Play back recorded samples of real instruments.
  • Drum Machines: Emulate drum sounds and patterns.
  • Orchestral Libraries: Provide realistic orchestral instrument sounds
  • Pianos and Keyboards: Emulate acoustic and electric pianos, organs, and other keyboard instruments.

Part 2: Loading a VST Instrument

Cubase supports VST (Virtual Studio Technology) instruments, allowing you to integrate virtual instruments into your projects seamlessly.

Steps to Load a VST Instrument:

Add an Instrument Track:

  • Click on the + icon above the Track list or press "T" to open the Add Track dialogue.
  • In the Instrument tab, choose a VST instrument from the list. For example, select Retrologue.
  • If you like, you can name the new Instrument Track in the name field, i.e. "Synthesizer".
  • Click Add Track.

Load the Instrument:

  • The selected VST instrument interface will open automatically.
  • In the VST instrument interface, choose a sound or preset you want to use.

Instrument plug-in window:

  • The instrument track will appear in the Project window, ready to be played.
  • You can close the Instrumente window if you don't need to see it.
  • You can find the loaded Instrument in the Routing Section of the Track Inspector.
  • You can open the plug-in window of the instrument by clicking on the picture or keyboard icon next to the name of the instrument.

Part 3: Recording a VST Instrument

Once you have loaded a VST instrument, you can record MIDI data to control it.

Steps to Record a VST Instrument:

Prepare for Recording:

  • Ensure your MIDI controller (e.g., keyboard) is connected and recognized by Cubase.
  • Select the instrument track you created in Part 2.

Arm the Track for Recording:

  • Click the Record Enable button on the instrument track. It looks like a red circle and should light up in red when activated.

Enable the Metronome (Optional):

  • Click the Metronome button in the Transport Bar to enable a click track for timing reference during recording.

Start Recording:

  • Position the playhead at the location where you want to start recording.
  • Press Record in the Transport Bar or press *** (asterisk)** on the numeric keypad.
  • Play your MIDI controller to record MIDI notes.

Stop Recording:

  • Press Stop in the Transport Bar or press Spacebar when you’re finished recording.

Review and Edit:

  • The recorded MIDI data will appear as an event in the instrument track.
  • Double-click the MIDI event to open the Key Editor where you can edit the MIDI notes, adjust velocities, and make other modifications.

Example: Lets's Record a Piano Part

Let’s say you want to record a piano part using a virtual instrument.

Load the VST Instrument:

  • Create an instrument track and select Halion Sonic.
  • In Halion Sonic, choose a piano preset, i.e. Jazz Grand Piano.
  • You can search for "Piano" in the MediaBay of HALion Sonic and select a preset in the result list.

Prepare for Recording:

  • Ensure your MIDI keyboard is connected.
  • Arm the instrument track for recording by clicking the Record Enable button.

Enable the Metronome:

  • Click the Metronome button in the Transport Bar for timing assistance.

Start Recording:

  • Position the playhead at the beginning of the project.
  • Press Record and play your piano part on the MIDI keyboard.

Stop Recording:

  • Press Stop when you’re done.

Review and Edit:

  • Double-click the recorded MIDI event to open the Key Editor.
  • Adjust any incorrect notes or change the velocities to improve the performance.

Tips for Using Virtual Instruments

Explore Presets: Most VST instruments come with a wide range of presets. Spend time exploring these to find sounds that inspire you.

Layer Sounds: Create a rich sound by layering multiple virtual instruments. For example, combine a piano with strings.

Use MIDI Controllers: Enhance your performance by using MIDI controllers to add expression and dynamics.

Quantize MIDI: Use the quantize function to correct the timing of your MIDI notes for tighter performances.

Automate Parameters: Automate VST instrument parameters to create dynamic changes in your sounds throughout your track.

By understanding virtual instruments and mastering the process of loading and recording them in Cubase, you’ll unlock endless possibilities for creating and shaping your music. These skills will help you produce professional-sounding tracks with a wide range of sounds and textures. Happy playing!
