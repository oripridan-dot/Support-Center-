This video looks at electric guitar production using SpectraLayers Elements, in particular correcting unwanted sounds. It takes power chords recorded on a Fender Stratocaster electric guitar, recorded through a Big Muff distortion and a Vox AC30 amplifier with a Shure SM57 microphone. Looking at the guitar chords with spectrum analyzing, a dominant octave can be identified and attenuated, letting an attractive guitar harmonic ring out. Fixing guitar harmonics and other frequencies with the spectrum editing in Spectralayers Elements is fast, easy and makes a huge difference to your sound.
