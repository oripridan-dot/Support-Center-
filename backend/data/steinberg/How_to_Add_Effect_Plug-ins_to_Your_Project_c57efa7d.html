How to Add Effect Plug-ins to Your Project

Download Cubase Welcome Tutorial Project

In this tutorial, we’ll explore how to add effect plug-ins to your project. You’ll learn about virtual effects and different ways to apply effect plug-ins using the MediaBay, Inspector Insert, and Channel Strip. You can load the "Cubase Welcome Tutorial" to learn how to work with effects.

Learning Goals:

  • Learn about virtual effects.
  • Learn about different ways to apply effect plug-ins.

Part 1: Understanding Virtual Effects

Virtual effects are software-based audio effects that you can apply to your tracks to alter their sound. They range from simple equalizers (EQ) and reverb to more complex effects like modulation and dynamic processing.

Common Types of Virtual Effects:

  • EQ (Equalization): Adjusts the balance of different frequency components.
  • Reverb: Simulates the sound of an acoustic environment, like a concert hall or a church.
  • Delay: Creates echo effects.
  • Compression: Controls the dynamic range of the audio.
  • Chorus, Flanger, and Phaser: Adds modulation effects for a richer sound.

Part 2: Applying Effect Plug-ins

Cubase provides multiple ways to apply effect plug-ins to your tracks. We’ll cover two main methods: using the Media Rack and Inspector Insert.

Method 1: Using the Inspector Insert

The Channel Tab is a panel on the left side of the Project window that provides the channel-related controls, like the volume fader and the effect section, for the selected track. You can show and hide it with zone buttons in the top right corner on the tool bar.

Select the Track:

  • Click on the track you want to add an effect to.

Open the Channel Tab:

  • If the Channel Tab is not visible, click Show Channel on the Project window toolbar in the top right corner (or press Command+Option+C).

Add an Insert Effect:

  • In the Channel, locate the Inserts section.
  • Click on an empty insert slot to open the effect plug-in menu.
  • Select the desired category and the effect plug-in from the list.
  • The effect is now inserted in the signal chain and the plug-in window will open automatically.  The audio signal passes through the insert effects from top to bottom, according to their slot positions. So if you have a delay in the first slot and a reverb in the second, the audio signal will pass through the delay first and then add the reverb on top. Of course you can always change the position of the effects by simply dragging them to a different position.

Remove an Insert Effect:

  • To remove an insert effect just click the effect in the Insert section and drag it out.

Method 2: Using the Media Rack

The MediaBay is a centralized location for managing your media files, including effect plug-ins.

Open the Media Rack in the Right Zone:

  • To open the Media rack in the right zone, click Show/Hide Right Zone on the Project window toolbar. At the top of the right zone, click the Media tab.

Locate Effect Plug-ins:

  • In the Media Rack, navigate to the VST Effects section. You’ll see a list of available effect plug-ins.

Drag and Drop:

  • Drag the desired effect plug-in from the MediaBay and drop it onto the track you want to apply it to in the Project window. The effect will be added to the first available insert slot. Easy, isn't it?

Exercise: Let's add effects to a Vocal Track

If you load the "Cubase Welcome Tutorial" and you can see a track called "Vocals" at the bottom. Let's start with that one.

Preparation tips

  • The vocals are the orange events at the bottom of the project. If you select them all with the selection tool and press "P" the locators will be placed in the same section as the vocals. Now activate the Cycle mode and you are ready to go.
  • You can use the "solo" button on the track to listen to the isolated track. Just click on the "S" icon to activate solo and click it again to deactivate it.

Bypass the existing effects

  • When you select the track and go to Insert section of the Channel Tab you will see that there is already an effect called "VocalChain" in the first slot. That's a great plug-in for vocals, but we want add our own mojo, so please move the mouse cursor on the effect slot and click on the bypass button on the left. The effect is now bypassed, meaning that it doesn't affect the signal any more. If you bypass it during playback you will hear the difference.

Let's add new effects

  • Click on the new empty slot and the plug-in list will open. There is a search field in the top right corner. Let's start with a reverb, so just enter "reverb" in the search and the list will show you all reverb plug-ins.
  • Choose "Roomworks" and the plug-in window will open automatically. If you play the project now you will hear a lot of reverb on the vocals. No worries. This is just the default setting.
  • You could now start tweaking the settings or start with a preset. The presets for Steinberg plug-ins can be loaded in the header of each plug-in. Click on the empty field and a list of presets will open. Let's try "Plate vintage".
  • Better, but still a lot of reverb, don't you think? Do you see the "Mix" knob on the right side of the plug-in? Reduce the mix amount a bit, maybe to 5%. How does it sound now? Also try the Reverb Time knob to adjust the reverb to the right amount.

Congratulations! You have just inserted and tweaked you first effect plug-in. Now have a look at the other tracks in the project and check out the different plug-ins that are used in the project.

Tips for Effective Use of Effect Plug-ins

Use Presets: Many effect plug-ins come with presets that can be great starting points. Try them out and tweak as needed.

Order Matters: The order of effects in the insert slots can affect the sound. Experiment with different orders to see what works best.

Keep it Subtle: Sometimes less is more. Avoid overloading your track with too many effects, which can clutter the sound.

By mastering the use of virtual effects and the different methods to apply them in Cubase, you’ll be able to enhance your tracks and achieve a polished, professional sound. Happy mixing!
